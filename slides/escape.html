Caveat Emptor

  • Not sure what a “respondent” is supposed to do…
  • I am just a practitioner, practicing “philosophy” as an amateur

What’s the Curse About?

  • Notation as a negotiation between a Human and a Machine
  • It can be more or less detached from the actual computing model (eg. abstract)

  • Notation as a negotiation between a “domain” and “computing”
  • Programmability dimension, how far can you depart from what’s pre-built in the notation
  • Problematic in itself: Domains do not exist in vacuum, they are also changed by their relation to machines

  • Notation as a representation of how humans talk about their domains
  • Domain Specific Languages, “trade jargon”

  • The dream of BDD: Expressing the “problem” in the language of the “domain experts”
  • DDD: using the language of the domain in the “solution space”
  • Removing intermediates (eg. Business Analysts, Product Managers)

  • Notation as way to negotiate meaning between humans
  • Famous Abelson & Sussman quote about reading vs. writing, human consumption
  • Programming is a translation process between “domains”, but it’s impure

Notations are Social Too

  • Taking the Linguistic approach seriously: Notations are social through and through
    • Structure of powers are embedded in notations and their use
  • Distiguishing “High-level” vs “Low-level” languages entail a certain world view
  • “Design” vs. “Implementation” reenacts the old dichotomy of thought vs. matter, thinking vs. doing, Poiesis vs. Praxis
  • Use of more or less complex formalisms to provide “semantics” to notations
  • “My language is bigger than yours” phenomenon, aka. C++ syndrom
  • Failure of separating Design (UML) from implementation (Code generation + some remains…) -> code fights back
